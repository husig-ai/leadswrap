Agent journey

From first enquiry to closed deal — in one place

This is what an agent's day looks like with Husig Close. Every step that used to be manual is either automated or made dramatically faster.

1
A lead comes in — from anywhere
Whether it's a Facebook lead ad, a WhatsApp message, an SMS to your property sign, someone you met at an open house, or a Zillow enquiry forwarded via Zapier — every lead lands in one place. Nothing slips through. You get a notification within seconds, with the lead's details and a Heat Score already calculated.
No more checking multiple platforms
2
The AI replies before you even open the app
Within 60 seconds of a new lead arriving, Husig Close sends a personalised first message on your behalf — using the lead's name and the property they asked about. "Hi Sarah, thanks for reaching out about 42 Oak Street. Are you looking to book a viewing or just gathering info?" You can turn this off any time, but most agents leave it on once they see it working.
Saves 15–30 min per new lead on first contact
3
You know exactly how serious they are
Every lead gets a Heat Score — a number from 0 to 100 based on six qualification signals: how specific their location preference is, how defined their budget is, how urgently they need to move, whether they're already working with an agent, how far along their mortgage is, and whether they've requested an appointment. The score updates every time they reply — if they say "I want to book a viewing this weekend," their score jumps immediately.
Stop wasting time on leads who are just browsing

When the lead replies — by SMS, WhatsApp, or email — you get a notification with a suggested reply already written, personalised to what they said and what you know about them. Tap Approve to send it. Tap Edit if you want to tweak it. Tap Discard if you want to write your own. You're always in control. The AI just does the first draft.
Saves 5–10 min per reply across an active pipeline

Full call history without extra note-taking

Saves 10–15 min per showing on back-and-forth scheduling

AI features

What the AI actually does — and what it doesn't

Every AI feature in Husig Close is designed around one rule: the agent is always in control. The AI drafts, suggests, and summarises — it never sends anything without your approval, except the very first message (which you can disable).

What we are not building in V2: AI voice assistants that answer calls on your behalf, automated AI-to-lead conversations without agent oversight, or any feature that removes human judgment from a financial transaction. Real estate is a high-trust business. The AI supports the agent — it does not replace them.
⚡
Speed-to-lead auto-responder
The moment a new lead comes in, Husig Close sends a personalised first message within 60 seconds — without any action from you. It uses the lead's name, the property they enquired about, and a friendly opening question to start the conversation. This single feature is arguably the highest-ROI thing in the product. The window where you're most likely to get a reply is the first 5 minutes. The AI owns that window for you.
Replaces: manually crafting a first message for every new lead
✍️
AI reply drafting (SMS, WhatsApp, email)
Every time a lead sends you a message — on any channel — Husig Close drafts a reply before you even open the thread. The draft is personalised using everything the system knows: what they asked about, what their budget is, what their urgency level is, and what you've already said to them. You review, edit if needed, and approve with one tap. The tone adjusts per channel — shorter and casual for SMS, more detailed for email.
Replaces: staring at a screen trying to figure out what to write back
🎯
Heat Score — live lead qualification
Every lead has a score from 0 to 100 that tells you how ready they are to buy. It's calculated from six qualification signals — location certainty, budget clarity, motivation, mortgage status, whether they're already with another agent, and appointment commitment. The score updates in real time every time the lead sends a message. If they say something that signals urgency, the score jumps. If they go quiet for a week, it drifts down. Your pipeline stays sorted by who needs your attention most.
Replaces: gut feel about who's worth calling back today
🏠
Smart property matching
Based on what a lead has told you — budget, location preference, property type, number of bedrooms, urgency — Husig Close ranks your property inventory and shows you the best matches. When the AI drafts a follow-up email or message, it pulls the top matching properties in automatically. The agent sees the draft with properties pre-populated and can swap or remove any of them before sending. It looks like you spent an hour researching. It took 10 seconds.
Replaces: manually browsing your listings to find what fits each lead
🎤
Voice lead capture
At an open house, you meet a prospect and have a 3-minute conversation. Instead of scrambling for a notepad or trying to remember it all later, you tap the mic in the app and speak naturally while you talk with them — or immediately after. Husig Close transcribes the conversation and extracts the key details: name, phone, budget, what they're looking for, their timeline. You get a pre-filled lead form to review and confirm. The full audio and transcript are stored on the lead record.
Replaces: business cards, notepads, and "I'll remember this one"
📝
Post-call notes (recorded calls only)
If you choose to record a call, Husig Close transcribes it after it ends and produces three things automatically: a 2-sentence summary of what was discussed, a sentiment tag (Motivated / Interested / Neutral / Hesitant), and an action items checklist extracted from the conversation — "Send floor plan," "Follow up Tuesday," "Check 3-bed availability." These are added to the lead's timeline. You can edit the summary before saving. No recording means no automatic notes — you fill in the outcome manually.
Replaces: post-call note-taking that nobody does consistently
💬
WhatsApp voice note transcription
Many leads — especially in markets outside the US — prefer sending WhatsApp voice notes over typing. Husig Close automatically transcribes these voice notes the moment they arrive. The transcript is stored as the message text, intent is extracted from it, and the AI drafts a reply based on what was said. From your perspective, a voice note works exactly like a text message — you read the transcript, review the draft, and reply.
No more playing a voice note three times trying to remember what was said

Lead sources & aggregator integration

Every place you get leads, feeding into one inbox

Real estate agents get leads from many places — and Husig Close brings them all together. But not every platform works the same way. Here is exactly what connects automatically, what requires a workaround today, and what is coming later.

Important note on Zillow and Realtor.com: Both platforms require CRM vendors to go through a formal certification process before they can receive lead data automatically. This is not a quick integration — it takes weeks to months to get approved, and both platforms reserve the right to decline. We are applying for both certifications. Until approved, agents can use a Zapier workaround to forward leads from those platforms into Husig Close. This is covered below.

Manual entry + voice capture

Add leads yourself via the quick-add form, or speak naturally after an open house conversation and let the AI extract the details. Always available, no dependencies.

Zillow and Realtor.com — how they actually work

Who owns the data: Zillow and Realtor.com own their lead data. To receive it automatically in a CRM, the CRM vendor must apply to each platform's partner programme and pass a review process. Until approved, the agent — not the CRM — is the only party that can access their own leads on those platforms. This is the same situation every CRM faces when they start out.

Zillow Premier Agent

Zillow's lead delivery programme is called Tech Connect. It is a paid advertising product — agents pay Zillow to be featured on listings in specific areas. Leads from those listings are delivered to a CRM only if the CRM is a certified Tech Connect partner.

Our status: certification application in progress. Expected to take 4–8 weeks from submission.

In the meantime: agents can forward Zillow leads to Husig Close via Zapier in about 10 minutes. The lead arrives with source tagged as "Zillow" and goes through the same pipeline — Heat Score, AI reply, everything. It is a workaround, not a permanent solution.

Realtor.com

Realtor.com has an equivalent programme called Connections Plus. Same model — agents pay for leads, and delivery to a CRM requires the CRM to be a certified partner.

Our status: certification application in progress, in parallel with Zillow.

In the meantime: same Zapier workaround applies. Realtor.com also has its own app that agents can manually forward leads from, and we are documenting both paths.

What the Zapier workaround looks like for agents: the agent logs into Zapier (free tier is sufficient), connects their Zillow or Realtor.com account, and sets up a one-click automation that sends new leads to Husig Close. Once set up, it runs automatically — the agent does not need to do anything else. It is slightly less seamless than a direct integration but functionally identical from that point forward.

MLS — why it is not in V2

MLS (Multiple Listing Service) is not one platform — it is 580+ regional databases across the US, each operated independently with different access rules and data licensing terms. There is no single "connect your MLS" button.

In V2, agents add their property listings manually or import them. This covers the matching and scheduling functionality that matters most. A direct MLS sync — via a third-party data aggregator — is planned for a later release once we have confirmed demand and selected the right partner.

What agents typically think of as "MLS leads" are actually delivered through Zillow, Realtor.com, and the portals built on top of MLS data — not from the MLS board directly. Those are covered above.

Heat Score

Know who to call — before you even open the lead

Heat Score is Husig Close's lead qualification engine. It gives every lead a score from 0 to 100 based on six signals drawn from the real estate industry's LPMAMA qualification framework. The score updates live as the lead provides more information through conversation.

📍

Location certainty

Do they know where they want to live? Have they specified a neighbourhood or are they open to anything? A lead with a specific location preference scores higher — they've done their homework.

💰

Budget clarity

Is the budget range narrow and certain, or wide and unsure? A lead who says "between $480k and $500k, pre-approved" scores far higher than "somewhere around $500k, not sure yet."

🏃

Motivation & urgency

Are they relocating for a new job next month, or just casually browsing? Urgency is one of the strongest signals. Relocations and first-home buyers score high. Browsing scores low.

🤝

Agent status

Are they already working with another agent? A lead without an agent is a real opportunity. One who's already committed is lower priority — though still worth nurturing.

🏦

Mortgage readiness

A cash buyer or pre-approved lead can move fast. Someone who hasn't started the mortgage process yet may be months away from being ready to offer. This is weighted heavily.

📅

Appointment intent

Have they asked for a viewing, or been scheduled for one? Nothing signals readiness like booking time in your diary. A booked appointment is the strongest single signal in the score.

🔥 Hot (75–100)

Call within the hour. This lead is qualified, motivated, and ready. Don't wait.

🌤 Warm (50–74)

Follow up today. Strong signals on most dimensions. One good conversation could move this forward.

❄️ Cool (25–49)

Nurture weekly. Not ready yet but worth keeping warm. Let the AI handle the drip.

🧊 Cold (0–24)

Long-term drip only. Very early stage or very low intent. Don't spend manual time here.

Open decisions

What we still need to resolve before building

These are not blockers — development can start while these are discussed. But they affect scope, design, and how we describe the product to agents.

Open Web app: Jinja/HTMX served from our backend vs Next.js as a separate frontend

Both options deliver the same 8-page web app to users. The difference is infrastructure complexity and the skill set required to maintain it.

Jinja + HTMX (simpler) Web app is served directly from our FastAPI backend. No separate deployment. No Node.js. Pages load server-side. HTMX handles dynamic interactions. Lower maintenance overhead. One EC2 instance covers everything.
Next.js (more capable) More flexible for complex UI interactions. Larger ecosystem of components. Easier to hand off to a frontend developer who knows React. Requires a separate deployment (Vercel or another EC2). More moving parts.

Our current recommendation: start with Jinja/HTMX for V2. The web app is a dashboard — not a rich interactive application. The complexity of Next.js is not needed yet. Revisit in V3 if the web app becomes a primary product surface.

Open Lead assignment in a team — manual only or automatic round-robin?

Today, leads are assigned to the agent who created them, or manually reassigned by the admin. For firms that get high inbound volumes from aggregators, manual assignment becomes a bottleneck.

Manual assignment (V2) Simpler. Admin has full control. No edge cases to handle (what if an agent is on holiday?). Works for most small teams.
Round-robin auto-assignment (V2 or V3) Eliminates admin overhead for high-volume firms. Requires rules around availability, capacity, and specialisation. More complex to build and explain.

Recommendation: ship manual assignment in V2. Add round-robin as a configurable option in V3 once we understand how firm admins actually assign leads in practice.

Open WhatsApp number — one per firm or one per agent?

Twilio allows WhatsApp Business numbers to be provisioned per account. The question is whether every agent in a firm gets their own WA number, or whether the firm shares one number with leads routed to the right agent.

One shared firm number (V2) Simpler to set up and manage. All inbound WA messages go to one place, then get assigned. Leads message the firm, not the individual agent. Lower cost.
One per agent (later) More personal — leads message their specific agent directly. Harder to manage at scale. More Twilio accounts and configurations.

Recommendation: one shared firm number for V2. Agents can have individual SMS numbers from day one.
